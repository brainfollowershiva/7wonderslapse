New 7 World Wonders

1.Great Wall of China : (China)

  • Description:

    • A massive fortification built to protect against invasions from nomadic groups. It stretches over 13,000 miles.

  • Construction:

    • Began in the 7th century BC and continued through the 16th century.

  • Significance:

    • An iconic symbol of China's historical strength and ingenuity.

2.Petra (Jordan):

  • Description:

    • An ancient city carved into red sandstone cliffs, known for its rock-cut architecture and water conduit system.

  • Construction:

    • Established as early as the 5th century BC and flourished as a major trading hub in the 1st century AD.

  • Significance:

    • Represents the ingenuity of the Nabataean civilization and is a significant archaeological site

3.Christ the Redeemer (Brazil):

  • Description:

    • A colossal statue of Jesus Christ overlooking Rio de Janeiro, standing 98 feet tall with arms outstretched.

  • Construction:

    • Completed in 1931.

  • Significance:

    • A symbol of peace and a major cultural landmark for Brazil.

4.Machu Picchu (Peru):

  • Description:

    • An ancient Incan city located in the Andes Mountains, often referred to as the "Lost City of the Incas."

  • Construction:

    • Built in the mid-15th century under Inca emperor Pachacuti

  • Significance:

    • A marvel of Incan engineering and a major tourist attraction known for its archaeological and cultural value.

5.Chichen Itza (Mexico):

  • Description:

    • A large pre-Columbian archaeological site built by the Maya civilization, famous for its pyramid, El Castillo."

  • Construction:

    • Major construction occurred between the 7th and 10th centuries AD.

  • Significance:

    • An important center of Maya culture, particularly known for its astronomical and architectural achievements.

6.Roman Colosseum (Italy):

  • Description:

    • An ancient amphitheater in Rome, known for its gladiatorial contests and public spectacles."

  • Construction:

    • Began in AD 72 and completed in AD 80.

  • Significance:

    • A symbol of the architectural and engineering prowess of ancient Rome and a major tourist destination.

7.Taj Mahal (India):

  • Description:

    • A stunning white marble mausoleum built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal."

  • Construction:

    • Completed in 1653.

  • Significance:

    • Renowned for its exquisite architecture and as a symbol of eternal love.
